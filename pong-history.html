The History of Pong

The Beginning

Pong is a broundbreaking game released by the game manufacturer Atari, Inc in 1972. It is one of the earliest video games and became very popular - launching the video game industry. Pong is a two dimensional game with two players who control paddles somewhat emulating a game of ping-pong. Players used the paddles to volley a ball between each other across the screen. If you miss the ball, you the opponent will win a point [source britannica.com - work required for referencing].

Ralph Baer proposed the idea of simple games people could play on their own television sets in 1958. Atari founder Nolan Bushnell created pong. The company sold more than 8,000 pong arcade machines. Pong then got added to a console and became a favourite of many people at home. Though the popularity of pong declined in the 1980s due to more complex games being made - we're renewing this games popularity.
